Mushtaq back at ease doing his level best for Sussex

17 february, 2007
Sport

The signing of Cristiano Ronaldo on a new five-year, $31 million contract yesterday represented the best possible news for a buoyant Manchester United as they continue their quest for success on three fronts. But the ink had barely begun to dry when Sir Alex Ferguson angrily condemned Real Madrid’s attempts to unsettle a player he has challenged to follow in the footsteps of Pele and Diego Maradona.

But the ink had barely begun to dry when Sir Alex Ferguson angrily condemned Real Madrid’s attempts to unsettle a player he has challenged to follow in the footsteps of Pele and Diego Maradona.

Ronaldo’s decision to sign a deal worth about $120,000 a week, making him the best-paid player in United’s history, came as a serious blow to Real, who had been encouraged to believe that the 22-year-old Portugal winger would join them in the summer. Those hopes are now over — for another year at least — but Ferguson’s delight did not stop him castigating the Spanish club for their part in the saga.
